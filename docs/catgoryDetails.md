2. Matches

The Matches section allows the club owner to create and manage all football matches related to the club’s teams. This includes scheduling upcoming matches, updating match details, and recording match results. The club owner can add information such as the opponent team, match date, time, and location, and assign the match to a specific team within the club. Once a match is created, players and members can view it in their team schedule and calendar, helping everyone stay informed about upcoming games. This section helps the club keep track of all competitive fixtures and ensures that players are properly notified and prepared for each match.

3. Updates

The Updates section is used by the club owner to share important announcements and official messages with club members. Unlike regular posts that can be created by different members, updates are typically formal communications from the club management. These updates help keep everyone informed about important changes or news related to the club. For example, the club owner might post updates such as changes to the training schedule, upcoming club meetings, new policies, or important notices for players and volunteers. This section ensures that critical information is communicated clearly and reaches all relevant members of the club.

4. Events

The Events section is used to organize and manage all club-related activities that are not necessarily official matches. These can include training sessions, club meetings, social gatherings, fundraisers, tournaments, or community activities. The club owner can create events by specifying details such as the event title, date, time, and location. Members of the club can view these events in the calendar and may also have the option to RSVP or receive reminders. This section helps the club coordinate activities efficiently and ensures that members stay aware of important club events.

5. Network

The Network section allows the club owner to interact with other clubs that are part of the same platform. Since the system supports multiple clubs, this feature enables clubs to discover, follow, and view public content from other clubs. Through the network section, the club owner can explore other clubs, follow them to stay updated on their public posts, and promote their own club within the wider community. For example, if one club follows another club, they can see public announcements, achievements, or events shared by that club. This feature helps build a broader community between clubs and encourages collaboration and visibility across the platform.
